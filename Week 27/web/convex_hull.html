Convex Hull (Andrew's Monotone Chain)

WEEK 27 - WEB (JS) ADVANCED TOPICS

CONCEPT: Smallest convex polygon containing all given points. Andrew's algorithm sorts by (x, y) and walks once left-to-right (lower hull) and once right-to-left (upper hull) using a cross-product test on the stack.

KEY POINTS: cross product orientation; pop on non-CCW triple; O(n log n).

ALGORITHM: sort points; build lower then upper hull; concatenate dropping duplicated endpoints.

JS-SPECIFIC NOTES: JS numbers are doubles; for integer-only inputs they're exact up to 2^53.

DRY RUN: {(0,0),(1,1),(2,2),(3,1),(0,3),(2,4)} -> hull {(0,0),(3,1),(2,4),(0,3)} (CCW).

COMPLEXITY: Time O(n log n); Space O(n).
