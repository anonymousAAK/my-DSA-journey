Dinic's Maximum Flow

WEEK 26 - WEB (JS) ADVANCED TOPICS

CONCEPT: Phase-based max-flow that builds a level graph (BFS) and pushes blocking flow (DFS with an iter[] dead-edge pointer). O(V^2 * E) and O(E*sqrt(V)) on unit graphs.

KEY POINTS: Per-phase BFS + repeated DFS; iter[] ensures each edge is touched at most O(1) times per phase.

ALGORITHM: repeat: BFS sets level[]; reset iter[]; DFS pushes blocking flow. Stop when sink is no longer reachable.

JS-SPECIFIC NOTES: Use typed arrays (Int32Array) for level/iter — they are faster and avoid the holes-in-arrays JS engine penalty.

DRY RUN: CLRS graph -> phase 1 sends 16, phase 2 sends 7, total 23.

COMPLEXITY: Time O(V^2 * E); O(E*sqrt(V)) on unit graphs. Space O(V + E).
